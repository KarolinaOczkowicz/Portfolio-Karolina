Costa. Buidling personalized experience.

The story of the iterative process driven by human feedback

    Problem

    The enthusiasts of different water sport disciplines need a clear, concise and visually appealing way to understand the weather conditions because they want to practice water sports safely.

    Solution

    I chose to share and present detailed weather forecasts that can get easily personalized by users so that they remain fully informed about the weather conditions for a specific location of interest and specific water discipline.

    My role

    • Product strategy including comptetitive analysis
    • User research
    • Persona creation
    • MVP definition
    • Wireframes
    • UI Design & Prototyping
    • Illustration/Icon design
    • Usability Testing

    Tools

    Pen & paper, Balsamiq, Sketch, Invision.

    Outcome

    Costa - personalized weather forecast web app for water sports fans

PROCESS

PLANNING.EXECUTION.MEASURING

I believe that research is an integral part of the design process and there is no good design without good research. Different research methods were applied to different stages of the design process. I started by drafting the timeline for these crucial moments when the research takes an active part and brings valuable insight into the product and its design process.

RESEARCH AND PROBLEM DEFINITION

HYPOTHESIS

The observations, survey and the interviews I conducted at the beginning of my assignment, led me to question the necessity of implementing global weather forecast maps into the design. The results of the above actions proved that the users are interested in checking one or a few particular locations only. It is very different to the method applied in the TV weather forecast with multiple users in front of a screen who are based in multiple locations. Moreover, global weather maps do not work well on mobile; they occupy a lot of space and show irrelevant data as users are interested in checking the weather forecast for particular locations. Consequently, I decided to exclude the weather forecast maps from the design at the very early stage of the design process.

PERSONAS

USERS.FIRST

Personas were created to synthesize & gather all the outcome of the interviews. The participants were recruited from the target group and I made sure that they meet previously defined criteria.

Design impact

Focussing on users and how they interact with the prototype helped me to understand the physical, enviromantal conditions as well as emotional state of the users at the moment of interaction. The findings led me to change the way I approached this project differentely:
  • One of the interesting findings was that different weather conditions affect different water sports.As a result I proposed building a personalized product where users can check weather conditions which directly affect a water sports discipline of the users´choice
  • The users are interested in checking one or a few particular locations only. It is very different to the method applied in the TV weather forecast with multiple users in front of a screen who are based in multiple locations. Moreover, global weather maps do not work well on mobile; they occupy a lot of space and show irrelevant data as users are interested in checking the weather forecast for particular locations. Consequently, I decided to exclude the weather forecast maps from the design at the very early stage of the design process

User stories and MVP

User research and persona creation brought up the users main needs, goals, and behaviours. Therefore, I found that the main issues my design decisions needed to solve were:

  • Allow the users to check the weather forecast by location and by water sport of their choice
  • Make it easy to understand the weather forecast data
  • Ability to save favourite locations
  • Let the users decide which weather conditions they want to see

Based on this information, I was able to create user stories and define the MVP. Building MVP allowed me to start the first iterations.

DESIGN EXPLORATIONS

Sketch.Anywhere

Once the problem was defined I could use my sketching skills to draft the solution. I used Balsamiq to design low fidelity wireframes based upon the user flows and Sketch to design high fidelity wireframes and mockups. This is when I could start the second research phase - making sure that the design is pleasant and easy to use by applying usability testing method. The low-fi prototype allowed me to iterate quickly and I could share my design and gather the very first feedback. The fully functioning prototyping was used when testing a more mature design.

TESTING PHASE

Test.Often

Someone once said that designing without testing is like getting into a cab and saying ¨just drive¨. I conducted several testing rounds that revealed possible usability problems and proved that human feedback is the king. Before starting to run the test I prepared the test plan which included e.g. the scope and the user tasks. I recruited the participants and conducted the test using the fully functioning prototype. When conducting the usability testing the ¨think aloud¨ method was applied and to evaluate the findings I used Jakob Nielsen’s scale. The testing and its results proved that there were parts of the navigation system that the users understand however there were some critical parts of the information architecture that were very problematic and the users did not know where to go to do critical tasks e.g.:

  • the users were expecting to have the weather parameters already preset once they chose a watersport (or watersports). Solution: default settings
  • Some users felt frustrated that they could choose one value only when selecting optimal weather conditions for receiving notifications. Solution: dual sliders

WRAPPING UP

What have I learned from this project?

When working on Costa I have learned to:

  • name the problem
  • search for the solution in people
  • test often
  • design to draft the solution

The design process is not over. More water sports and non-weather alerts can be added to keep on improving the experience.
