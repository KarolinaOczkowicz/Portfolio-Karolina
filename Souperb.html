Souperb - food delivery app.

    Problem

    The restaurant ¨Souperb¨ needs an app that would help its users to order food online and have it delivered to the indicated address.

    Solution

    Souperb is a mobile app designed to optimize the food ordering process and increase sales. Its extended functionality allows users to order a soup and before placing an order check description, ingredients and reviews of other users.

    My role

    • Market research & SWOT Analysis
    • Persona creation
    • MVP definition
    • Low-Mid-High Fidelity Wireframes
    • UI Design & Prototyping
    • UI Design

    Tools

    Pen & paper, Balsamiq, Sketch, Flinto.

    PROCESS

    Competitive analysis

    I started the design process by conducting user and market research. Based on the findings I created SWOT analysis for the Deliveroo app.

    Strengths

    • Free
    • Good UX Design & easiness of use
    • Recognizable brand
    • Variety of restaurants
    • Good ratings (4.7 on AppleStore)

    Weaknesses

    • Geographically limited
    • No onboarding for new users
    • Few filters for browsing the content (Gluten free-Organic-Vegan-Vegetarian)

    Opportunities

    • Onboarding
    • Rapidly increasing online food delivering market.
    • Increase the number of filters

    Threats

    • Existing competitors on the market e.g. Just Eat or Glovo which offers cheaper services.
    • Delivery limitations across the city.
    • High price

    Personas

    Personas were created to synthesize & gather all the outcome of the interviews. Focussing on the users helped me to understand the physical, environmental conditions as well as the emotional state of the users at the moment of ordering the food online. The findings led me to change the way I approached this project. I decided to interview the family of Javier to find out how people order food online when being in a group and if being in a group affects the decisions they make. Below some of the paint points Javier and his family encountered when ordering food:

    • You can not deliver the same order to multiple addresses.
    • When Javier´s wife places an order using her mobile then it´s in her ordering history: ¨We share the same dinner but we have to switch mobiles when checking order history and repeating the same order. There is no family account option".
    • Trust issues such as food quality, payment security and transparency, delivery time.

    User Flow

    I mapped out the users’ steps to see how I could simplify their journey to help them reach their most important goal with the app, always having on mind the users´ best interest and business goal.

    Sketches

    The knowledge about my users and their goals influenced the sketches and the design. Having in mind that I´m working on a native app I arranged the elements following the iOS guidelines. The sketch allowed me to move forward with the design and create low fidelity wireframes.

    The second step was to create wireframes for testing purposes. Low fidelity wireframes helped me to gather the first feedback on the design. I used Balsamiq to develop super-low fidelity wireframes.

    Final design

    As for the color palette in the app, I played with the contrast of backgrounds and the consistency of colors is used for all touchable areas. The graphics look stylish and elegant. Motion attracts human attention. In Souperb the little detail - evaporating dish - invites to place an order.
